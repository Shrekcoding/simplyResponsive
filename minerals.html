Silver

Silver is described as a soft, white, lustrous transition metal. Its atomic number is 47 and its symbol is Ag, which in latin is short for Argentum which means "shiny White" in English. To this day, silver exhibits the highest electrical contuctivity, thermal conductivity, and reflectivity of any metal which makes it extreemly valuable for industrial work and mirror making. Most silver is produced as a byproduct of copper, gold, lead, and zinc refining. As a form of currency, silver can be invested in the form of silver dollars. It can cost anywhere from 10-100 dollars to invest in a single silver dollar. Silver can also be used as jewelry in the form of necklasses, rings, bracelets and watches. In the famous James Bond films, you can often see the main character, James bond, rocking his silver watch to mach his classy tuxedo Besides being a form of jewelry and currency, silver can be found in everyday objects such as solar panels, water filtration units, and table utensils "hence the term silverware". Oxygen

Oxygen is what animals inhale and what plants exhale, and is the Chemical element with the symbol O and the atomic number of 8. Oxygen is a highly reactive nonmetal that can have devastating results when set ablaze. oxygen is Earth's most abbundant element, and is the third most abbundant element in the universe. Most of the mass in a living organism is oxygen in fact, 60% of the human body is oxygen. One of the first known experiments on the relationship between combustion and air was conducted by the 2nd century BCE Greek writer on mechanics, Philo of Byzantium. In his work Pneumatica, Philo observed that inverting a vessel over a burning candle and surrounding the vessel's neck with water resulted in some water rising into the neck. Philo incorrectly surmised that parts of the air in the vessel were converted into the classical element fire and thus were able to escape through pores in the glass. Many centuries later Leonardo da Vinci built on Philo's work by observing that a portion of air is consumed during combustion and respiration. Oxygen is too chemically reactive to remain a free element in air without being continuously replenished by the photosynthetic action of living organisms. Another form (allotrope) of oxygen, ozone (O3), strongly absorbs ultraviolet UVB radiation and the high-altitude ozone layer helps protect the biosphere from ultraviolet radiation. However, ozone present at the surface is a byproduct of smog and thus a pollutant.

Such are the reasons as to why some people say blood smells like copper or why it feels like you mouth is full of copper after a hard workout. Copper does not react to water the way other metals do. That being said, copper is known to oxidise and corrode when exposed to oxygen for an extended period of time. copper, when oxidised, turns from a bright orange-brown to a sort of teal greenish gray. This is why the stautue of liberty is seen as green as opposed to brownish red. To prevent copper from oxidising, one may apply wax to copper as the wax acts as a shield to the effects oxygen has on copper. It also makes the copper shiny, which is a bonus.
